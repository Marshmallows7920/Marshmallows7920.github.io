Intro

Music has been my hobby since I was very little. I have been to almost 10 schools and have tried to learn different type
of music at each school. The majority of musical instruments can be put into 5 different categories:

  1. Wind Instruments

    • Commonly referred to as "The WoodWinds" because
      many wind instruments use a thin piece of
      wood called a reed, which vibrates when you
      blow across it to produce the sound

  2. Brass Instruments
  3. String Instruments
  4. Percussion
  5. Keyboard

Every instrument cannot be put into a category because music can be made out of anything, that's why I enjoy it.

The Start of My Musical
Journey

My music journey starts at a small school in Brampton,
Ontario. I had just arrived in Canada and was able
to start school the following week. In music class,
everyone was learning how to play the piano and had
already got the basics of international music notation.
This was all new to me beacuse in India the used words
to represent music, not a proper notation. The music
teacher helped me understand the notation that the other
students were learning. I did not get a chance to try out
the music I had composed because school ended in a couple
of weeks. During the summer, my father got a job in a
different city and I had to leave everything behind and
move to Winnipeg.

My Recorder Life

I went into grade three at Victor Major, already knowing the
international music notation. I was in a new school, in a
new city and a new province. They had a completly different
curriculum. Most students coming from grade 2 had never even
picked up an instrument. Since I was ahead of the class in
my musical knowledge, my teacher let me play the instruments
before the other students. I started out learning to play
the xylophone and than moving onto soprano recorder. After
about 4 years and 3 more schools later, I was at a very high
of recorder music. I was so far ahead, my grade 6 teacher
level stopped giving me music for the soprano recorder and
told me to try the "Alto". The alto recorder was a size
bigger but I quickly caught on. By the time more students
started to upgrade to the alto recorder, I had moved onto
the Tennor. It was even bigger. I wanted to try the base
but the instrument itself was bigger than me at the time.
The tennor recoreder was very easy to play because it had
the same fingering as the soprano. The only differencer was
that because of its size and the low rich sound it produced,
the notation was in the bass clef instead of the treble
clef which I had used for 4 years till grade 6.

Learning Religious Music

After learning all those differnt instruments and playing
styles, I wanted to try something differnt and go into
percussion. I asked my parents if they would allow me to
start playing drums. Ignoring all my previous musical
experience my parents sent me to learn religious music to
get "experience" before starting drums. I lost experience
due to the Indian musical notaion. I started going every
saturday to learn religious instruments and hymns. The
"experience" had almost no advantage or disadvantage. I
learned to sing and play the harmonium but the notation
was all words and I had to retrain myself and study the
mordern international notation again when I went back to
other instruments.

Junior High/High School Band and Beyond

Going into grade 7, I switched schools again and had to
forget the 5 years I had spent learning the various
different types of recorders and choose an instrument for
junior high band. I was fammiliar with the bass clef from
when I had played the Tenor recorder and wisely chose the
biggest instrument there was. I ended up with the BBb Tuba
which has a more rich sound than the normal tuba which can
also be in the style of a baritone. I was in a big band
class and the only Tuba in a band of 80. My main job was
keep the whole band together, playing deep long notes and
steady beats. My instrument is arguably one of the harder
brass instruments because of all the air it requires. It
required a lot of effort to get air through the whole
instrument. It was even harder to produce a sound loud
enough to be noticiable in the band of 80 students.
Once I was able to play the tuba, I was able to play
the other brass instruments with ease. They required less
effort but had the same fingering. This includes the
Euphonium which is like a smaller BBb Tuba and the Trumpet
which is an even smaller BBb Tuba. My Tuba career ended
when i changed schools for the 9th time. I ended up going
to a private catholic school called St Maurice. Starting
grade 9, I joined the highschool band. Coming from a band
of 80 students and joining a group of 4 was very diffucult
because I would play "too loud". Still, I finally had a
chance to play the drums which I had wanted to since grade
3. My hopes and dreams crashed again when the band program
was dropped due to a lack of participants. This was almost
the end of my music career but I joined a private band
outside school.
